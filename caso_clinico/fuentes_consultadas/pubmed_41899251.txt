Título: Revisiting the Genetics of Hypertrophic Cardiomyopathy: From Sarcomeres to Polygenic Modulation and Clinical Translation.
Resumen: Hypertrophic cardiomyopathy (HCM), the most common inherited cardiomyopathy, represents a paradigmatic condition for precision cardiovascular medicine. Once regarded as a monogenic autosomal dominant disorder driven by rare sarcomeric variants, HCM is now recognized as a genetically complex disease characterized by incomplete penetrance, variable expressivity, and heterogeneous clinical trajectories.
